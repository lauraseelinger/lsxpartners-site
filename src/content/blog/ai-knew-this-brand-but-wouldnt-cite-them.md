I'm the AI marketing nerd who looks forward to the monthly reporting to see what's happened across my clients. 🤓

For one of my clients, AI has known exactly who they are for months — even before we implemented any AI optimization. You could ask any platform about their category and the brand got named as the answer — but when you looked at where the citation actually pointed, it was always third-party sites and directory pages. They owned the category in AI's mind but had zero control over the content being cited.

A few months of foundation work later (some "common-sense when you really think about it" updates across the website — the stuff I outline in my AI optimization strategies for clients), the brand's own domain became the number one cited source for its category. One evergreen page we retrofitted with schema saw a 450% increase in how often AI pulled from it. And referral traffic from one AI platform increased nearly 9x in a month.

While some of these wins overlapped with a Google Core Update, I can defend the strategy and mechanism. Because nobody can guarantee AI citations, but you CAN structure everything so the probability moves in your favor. And then you get to watch closely and report back ballin' numbers like this.

And what's even better than getting an AI optimization strategy handed to you to implement is getting an AI visibility blueprint during a new website redesign. That means you're structurally optimized for AI from the ground up on the website instead of having to make little updates across the entire thing.

## AI optimization strategies change fast

When I first published my AI visibility audit guide, I told you the tools and process I was using in April. I also told you this space moves fast enough that I'd already swapped parts of my toolkit more than once. Two months later, that's still true, so I updated the guide to match what I'm actually doing now.

A few of the things that changed in less than two months:

- **Schema impact has shifted.** FAQPage rich results were retired in May, which means schema isn't about earning a visual search feature anymore. Its whole value now is making your content machine-readable so AI can extract and cite it.
- **Platform citations are clearer.** Reddit is still the most-cited domain across AI platforms (around 40% of citations in the most recent cross-platform analysis, and as high as 46.7% on Perplexity specifically). But the "YouTube > Reddit" data is also real. These platform weights are constantly shifting and platform-specific is the only way to think about it.
- **I added citation conversion tracking.** Because it's not just whether the count is growing, it's whether it brings traffic and sales to your brand. If you only watch the citation count, you'll miss the signal that tells you AI is becoming a real traffic driver.

## Owning the answer vs. owning the citation

This is the distinction most brands miss. AI naming you is not the same as AI crediting you. You can be the answer everyone gets and still send every click to a directory page or a competitor comparison you didn't write. The work is structuring your own content so that when AI reaches for a source, it reaches for you.

You can't guarantee a citation. You CAN load the dice.
