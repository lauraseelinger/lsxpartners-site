The Blueprint
The foundation that makes Kwikset's own pages the source AI cites.
The sitemap fixed where things live. This fixes how each page is built so AI can read it and pull from it. The same structuring also feeds Kai.
How this pairs with the sitemap
The sitemap is the IA layer — the nav tree, what's a page vs. a filter. This Blueprint is the layer underneath: the foundation (site-wide), the universal page rules, and a template per page type. Two linked deliverables, not one.
How to read it
  • Universal page rules — the rules every page follows, each with a diagram.
  • Foundation — the site-wide work the templates sit on.
  • Page templates — the sidebar lists every page type (the work map). Pick one → its spec + FAQ checklist + a visual of where the work goes.
  • Rollout sequences the build.
  • Additional strategies — off-site / earned-presence and ongoing moves to consider, beyond the on-site build.
What's "new" vs. "AI optimized"
Most of what's needed isn't net-new. The shells exist in the Figma/plan (Works-With, FAQ blocks, variants, Complete the Look), and the page types Hanson has presented to the client — Security hub, ecosystem "works with" pages, by-need / "best [lock] for" guides, multifamily/UNITE. The Blueprint’s job is AI-optimizing them: extractable text, schema, and the front-loaded answer copy. The few genuinely net-new pieces are the curated comparison content and two foundation items the latest sitemap surfaced — canonical strategy and llms.txt. 7 · SEO and AEO, balanced. Q: What deadbolt should I get for my front door?
A: For a main entrance, choose ANSI/BHMA Grade 1 — the highest residential rating — for forced-entry resistance. Then pick how you want to lock it (keyed, keypad, or smart), and a SmartKey model to rekey it yourself in seconds. Grade 2 is fine for lighter-duty exterior doors.
Questions to surface:

  • smart vs. regular deadbolt — more secure?
  • are keypad deadbolts safe
  • rekey to my existing key
Audience intelligence insight Kwikset makes Grade-1 deadbolts — the same top residential rating Schlage carries — but AI answers rarely credit it, and competitors own “high-security lock.” Stating the grade plainly, up top, is how the page closes that gap.

Q: Knob or lever — and which function do I need?
A: Knobs and levers are the latching hardware — pick by function first: keyed entry (exterior), privacy (bed/bath), passage (halls/closets, no lock), or dummy (pull-only). Levers are easier to operate. If the door already has a deadbolt, the knob or lever can be non-locking; then choose finish and grade.
Questions to surface:
  • passage, privacy, or keyed — which do I need for which door?
  • can I use an interior knob or lever on an exterior door?
  • do I need a locking knob if the door already has a deadbolt?
  • what lever or knob pairs with a smart lock / keypad deadbolt?
  • how do I match the knob and deadbolt — same brand, keyed alike?
Audience intelligence insight The recurring confusion isn't products, it's function — which lock type goes on which door (passage / privacy / keyed), whether an interior knob can go on an exterior door, and whether a knob even needs to lock when there's a deadbolt. Answer the function question first.

Q: Do I need a handleset, or a deadbolt and knob?
A: A handleset combines a deadbolt with a gripping handle for a front door. The thing to get right is fit — match your door's hole spacing (the center-to-center measurement) — plus the finish, and keep it keyed alike with your other locks so one key works everywhere.
Questions to surface:
  • do I need a handleset, or just a deadbolt and knob?
  • how do I find a replacement handleset that fits my door's hole spacing (center-to-center)?
  • can it be keyed alike to my existing deadbolt and other locks?
  • can I get a keypad handleset with a matching keyed handle?
Audience intelligence insight The loudest — and most ownable — pain is replacement fit: buyers can't find a handleset that matches their door's hole spacing (the center-to-center / 11-inch measurement). A fit + keyed-alike guide is the real content gap.
