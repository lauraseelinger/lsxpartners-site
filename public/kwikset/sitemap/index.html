Kwikset Navigation — Recommendations on the Working Sitemap

LSX recommendations on the working Kwikset sitemap (Miro, 6/16), backed by audience and search data.

The map
What to change in the sitemap, and why.
Buyers reach Kwikset through AI and search before the brand. Each change makes the structure match how people look — and gives AI a Kwikset page to cite. Detail per area in the tabs.
The approach
Buyers are choosing locks inside AI answers and search results before they ever land on Kwikset — and right now those answers are written by competitors and third-party listicles. This sitemap is built to take that back: grounded in how people actually talk about locks in the communities and reviews where they decide, pressure-tested against what all five AI engines say today, and structured so the pages AI cites are Kwikset's own. Search volume sizes the opportunity; the audience and the citation gaps decide the moves.

Comparison — a cross-cutting open question two types
Two comparisons that do different jobs — both static + schema-marked, since a dynamic widget can't be read or cited by AI:
Kwikset vs Kwikset — helps the shopper pick the right model (Halo vs Halo Touch, SmartCode vs Halo). It's both a conversion driver in the catalog and an AI-answer play: people ask AI "I'm headed to Lowe's today to grab a lock for my front door — which one do I actually need?" exactly so they don't have to read or decode a spec chart — so it has to be content AI can answer from, not just a visual table. Lives in Products per the comps.
Kwikset vs competitors — the citation play. On "Kwikset vs Schlage / Yale," Kwikset is mentioned but not cited, so a competitor or third-party page writes the comparison. In the audit, those pages are third-party — Reddit threads, locksmith blogs, Consumer Reports, Wirecutter — and the AI answer gets built from them, not from Kwikset. Ask the trust question — "what do locksmiths recommend?" — and Schlage, Medeco, and Mul-T-Lock come back: Kwikset is left out of Google's answer entirely, and graded down to budget / "entry-level" in chat. That's the citation an owned Kwikset page should be winning. An owned page that states grade parity + the rekey advantage and reframes the "cons" becomes the source AI cites — and keeps the consideration-stage shopper from bouncing off-site. Surface it in the security / ecosystem sections.

Answering the board's Products questions
Where do copy blocks go — top or bottom of the page? Front-load the answer: the first ~100–200 words carry the AI-citation weight, so a concise intro/answer goes up top with the deeper marketing blocks below. Why its own nav (vs. nested under Help Me Choose): "by need" is a distinct shopping mode from "by product," it's the highest-demand entry, and a top-nav item keeps it from getting buried like Style/Innovation. Help Me Choose still keeps Innovation + Buying Guides. SEMrush US, June 2026.

How the room / need destinations get built (page vs. filtered shelf): every room click lands on the same kind of result — a clean, titled grid of relevant locks. The high-demand ones (front door, garage, interior — 4,400–5,400/mo) get a stable URL + a short intro + schema so AI and search can cite them; the low-demand ones (halls/closets, gates — ~590) are filtered views of the catalog. The shopper sees one consistent experience; only the back-end retrievability differs, and the distinction is never surfaced in the UI. One product always keeps one canonical PDP, surfaced through every path (by type, by room, Smart & Keyless) — never duplicated per path.

The "Best smart lock for…" set is new — pages I recommend, not on the current Miro. They answer the unbranded "best for [front door / no hub / Apple Home / first-time homeowner]" searches where Kwikset is absent from AI today. "First-Time Homeowners," for example, goes to a retrievable guide that points a first-time buyer to the right Kwikset lock — same pattern for each.

Still in process
Open questions + what the full Blueprint builds out.
Below: what's still open for the team to decide, and the content-layer work that gets built out in the full Blueprint.
Open — for the team to land
How do shoppers reach the full catalog?
"Products" is the first top-nav item in the Figma comps but isn't on the Miro high-level — reconcile the two and confirm the catalog's entry point.
Where does About Us / Brand live?
Recommend it in the footer / utility nav, built as a structured "About Kwikset" entity page. It's not a shopping path, so it doesn't earn top-nav — but the content is an AI asset: an Organization-schema page that states what AI keeps getting wrong (current ownership — ASSA ABLOY / HHI, not Spectrum Brands; #1 US residential lock brand; SmartKey; Matter; the awards), so Kwikset's own page becomes the source AI cites when it describes the brand. Crosslink it to the Security hub and the awards. The other open page with an AI angle is on-site Search — it ties into Kai.
Collections & series — where do they go?
Treat them as merchandising, not a primary nav axis. Aura Reach, Halifax, Halo Select Plus, "signature / security series" are branded groupings with low search demand — surface them as a "shop by collection / finish" filter and through Get-the-Look, not a top browse path. (Largely the team's merchandising call; this is where I'd land.)
Built out in the full Blueprint — content & on-page layer
How the pages link to each other — concept pillars (security, ecosystem, move-in/rekey, multifamily) that link down to products and guides and back up, plus related-product links like closet → knob. The direction's set here; the full link map comes in the Blueprint.
The Support knowledge base — building the FAQs into a real Q&A library: the high-demand questions as their own answer pages (what AI quotes), the rest as categorical listings, all linked up into the shopping pages.
The comparison strategy — which competitors and attributes to cover, how to structure it so AI cites it, and how to keep it accurate as the catalog changes.
Page templates + the schema in them — PDP, category, and hub templates, with the answer put up top where AI grabs it.
PDFs into real pages — install guides and spec sheets are PDFs today, which AI can't read; the strategy prioritizes the highest-demand ones to convert first so they become citable.
Surfacing the awards — Halo Select Plus (IoT Breakthrough Smart Lock of the Year, 2026), Halifax (Good Housekeeping 2026), KwikSlide (Golden Hammer) — awards are a trust signal AI leans on, so they should show up on the security, comparison, and product pages.
Brand-signal cleanup — the facts AI currently has wrong about Kwikset (outdated ownership, missing SmartKey / Matter) and how to correct them.
Post-launch — the ongoing opportunity
KOL / third-party authority program — builder & locksmith voices on LinkedIn and in the communities AI reads, each turned into a citable case study. This is what flips the locksmith and Reddit citations above into Kwikset's favor over time.
Ongoing AI-visibility tracking — re-run the audit on a cadence to measure citation share as the new content lands, and re-prioritize from there.
Content activation — turning the priority topics into the published pages, guides, and third-party content, written in buyer language.

These sit beyond the launch Blueprint — the ongoing work that keeps compounding once the foundation is in place. It's a starting list, not the full set; more will surface as the audit re-runs and the market shifts.
